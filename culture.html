Culture of Bangladesh

Festivals, food, music, and traditions that bring people together

🎉

Pohela Boishakh

The Bengali New Year, celebrated every April with colorful street parades, music, and traditional food. It's one of the biggest cultural celebrations in Bangladesh, celebrated by people of all religions together.

🍛

Food

Rice and fish are at the heart of Bengali meals — the phrase "mach-e bhat-e Bangali" means "fish and rice make a Bengali." Popular dishes include biryani, hilsa fish curry, and sweets like roshogolla and mishti doi.

🎵

Music

Bangladesh has a rich musical tradition, including Baul folk songs (from wandering mystic singers), Rabindra Sangeet (songs written by Rabindranath Tagore), and modern Bangla band music.

💃

Dance

Traditional dance forms like Manipuri and folk dances are performed during festivals and cultural events, often telling stories through movement and expressive gestures.

👗

Clothing

Sarees (worn by women) and panjabi/lungi (worn by men) are traditional outfits, often made from Bangladesh's famous fine cotton and muslin fabric, historically prized around the world.

📖

Language & Literature

Bangla is the national language and one of the most spoken languages in the world. Bangladesh takes deep pride in its language, honored every year on International Mother Language Day (21 February).

🕌

Festivals

Along with Pohela Boishakh, Bangladesh celebrates Eid-ul-Fitr, Eid-ul-Adha, Durga Puja, and Christmas — reflecting the country's mix of religions living and celebrating together.

🎨

Arts & Crafts

Nakshi kantha (embroidered quilts), terracotta art, and rickshaw painting are unique Bangladeshi art forms passed down through generations, often made by hand in villages.

🏏

Sports

Cricket is the country's biggest passion, drawing huge crowds for every national match. Kabaddi is officially the national sport, while football also has a large and devoted following.
