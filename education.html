Education in Bangladesh

From free primary school to universities — how learning works across the country

The School Ladder

Every student in Bangladesh follows roughly this path

Age 6–10

Primary School (Grades 1–5)

Free and compulsory education for all children, provided by government, non-government, and religious (madrasah) schools. Textbooks are given out free every year.

Age 11–13

Junior Secondary (Grades 6–8)

Still part of free, compulsory education under Bangladesh's constitution, covering subjects like Bangla, English, Math, Science, and Social Studies.

Age 14–15

Secondary (Grades 9–10)

Students choose a track — Science, Business Studies, or Humanities. This stage ends with the SSC (Secondary School Certificate) public exam.

Age 16–17

Higher Secondary (Grades 11–12)

Two more years of study, usually at a college, ending with the HSC (Higher Secondary Certificate) exam — the main gateway into university.

Age 18+

University & Higher Education

Bachelor's degrees typically take 4 years (Honours) at one of Bangladesh's public and private universities, followed by optional Master's and PhD study.

Key Things to Know

How the system is organized behind the scenes

📚

NCTB

The National Curriculum and Textbook Board creates the curriculum and prints free textbooks used by almost every school in the country, distributed to over 40 million students each year.
🕌

Madrasah Education

A parallel religious education system running alongside general schools, with its own stages: Ebtedayee, Dakhil, Alim, Fazil, and Kamil, roughly matching primary through master's level.
🏛️

Dhaka University

Founded in 1921, it's the oldest and most prestigious university in Bangladesh, often called the "Oxford of the East."
